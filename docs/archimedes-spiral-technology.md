# Archimedes Spiral Technology

## Introduction
The Archimedes Spiral wind turbine represents a revolutionary approach to wind energy generation, inspired by the ancient Archimedean screw principle discovered over 2,000 years ago.

## Design Principles

### The Archimedes Spiral
The design is based on a conical spiral that rotates around a central axis. Unlike traditional horizontal-axis wind turbines (HAWT), the Archimedes Spiral features:

- **Conical Shape**: Tapered design from base to tip
- **Spiral Blades**: Multiple helical blades wrapped around the cone
- **Vertical Orientation**: Can be mounted vertically or at an angle
- **Omnidirectional**: Captures wind from any direction

## Technical Advantages

### 1. Efficiency
- Operates efficiently at wind speeds as low as 2 m/s
- Peak efficiency at moderate wind speeds (5-10 m/s)
- Maintains consistent power output across varying wind conditions

### 2. Safety
- Enclosed blade design reduces risk to wildlife
- Lower rotational speeds compared to traditional turbines
- Safer for bird and bat populations

### 3. Noise Reduction
- Significantly quieter operation (< 45 dB at 10m distance)
- Suitable for residential and urban environments
- Reduced mechanical stress and vibration

### 4. Durability
- Robust structure withstands extreme weather conditions
- Lower mechanical stress on components
- Reduced maintenance requirements
- Longer operational lifespan (25+ years)

## Specifications

### Standard Model (PKT-AS-2026)
- **Rotor Diameter**: 1.8 meters
- **Height**: 3.5 meters
- **Rated Power**: 3 kW
- **Cut-in Wind Speed**: 2 m/s
- **Rated Wind Speed**: 8 m/s
- **Survival Wind Speed**: 50 m/s
- **Annual Energy Production**: 4,500 - 7,000 kWh (depending on location)
